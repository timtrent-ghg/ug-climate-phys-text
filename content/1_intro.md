Welcome to *Climate Physics* — an undergraduate textbook covering the physical
principles that govern Earth's climate system. The book combines mathematical
theory, observational insight, and hands-on Python modelling.

## What This Book Covers

The text is organised into three parts:

**Part I — Radiation & Energy Balance**
builds from first principles of electromagnetic radiation through to the
greenhouse effect and climate feedbacks.

**Part II — The Atmosphere**
covers the vertical structure of the atmosphere, thermodynamic stability,
and the large-scale circulation patterns that distribute heat around the globe.

**Part III — The Ocean**
examines thermohaline circulation, ocean heat transport, and modes of
natural climate variability including ENSO.

## How to Use This Book

Each chapter page contains:

- **Narrative text** with derivations and physical intuition
- **Display mathematics** rendered via LaTeX, e.g. the Stefan–Boltzmann law:

$$
F = \sigma T^4
$$

- **Python code cells** that can be run interactively — click the 🚀 rocket
  icon at the top of any notebook page to open it in Google Colab.
- **Admonition boxes** for definitions, worked examples, and key results:

```{admonition} Definition
:class: tip
A **blackbody** is an idealised object that absorbs all incident radiation
and emits radiation solely as a function of its temperature.
```

- **Citations** in author–year format, e.g. {cite}`Pierrehumbert2010`, linked
  to the full bibliography.
